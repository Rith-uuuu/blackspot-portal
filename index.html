What Is an Accident Black Spot?

An accident black spot is a location on a road where a disproportionately high number of traffic accidents occur. Chennai, one of India's largest metro cities, has several such zones identified by the Tamil Nadu police and traffic authorities.

These spots are typically characterized by poor road design, inadequate signage, heavy traffic volume, or dangerous driver behavior. Regular monitoring and public awareness are critical tools in reducing fatalities at these locations.

This portal presents known black spots across Chennai with data sourced from traffic authority records, allowing citizens to stay informed and submit new hazard reports.

Road Safety Tips

🚗
Always wear your seatbelt. Both front and rear passengers must buckle up before the vehicle moves.
📵
Never use a mobile phone while driving — even hands-free calls reduce reaction time significantly.
🚦
Obey all traffic signals. Red lights at Chennai's busy intersections like Koyambedu are strictly enforced.
🍺
Never drive under the influence. Drunk driving is one of the top causes of night-time accidents on OMR and ECR.
⏱
Maintain safe following distance. Use the 3-second rule to ensure adequate braking time in heavy traffic.
🌧
Chennai's monsoon season makes roads extremely slippery. Reduce speed significantly during rains.
🌙
Use headlights correctly at night and switch to low beam for oncoming traffic on Chennai's highways.
🏍
Two-wheeler riders must wear ISI-certified helmets. Chennai sees a disproportionate share of two-wheeler fatalities.
🚛
Be cautious around heavy vehicles, especially on GST Road and NH-48 where trucks frequently change lanes.
🗺
Be extra cautious at identified black spots. Reduce speed and stay alert when passing known accident zones.
