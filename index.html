What Is an Accident Black Spot?

An accident black spot is a specific location on a road or highway where a disproportionately high number of traffic accidents occur compared to similar road sections. These zones are considered extremely dangerous due to recurring patterns of collision, injury, and fatality.


Black spots are identified through careful analysis of historical accident data, road geometry, traffic volume, and environmental factors. Understanding these zones allows authorities and planners to implement targeted safety measures to save lives.

Road Safety Tips

🚗
Always wear your seatbelt — both front and rear passengers must buckle up before the vehicle moves.
📵
Never use a mobile phone while driving. Even hands-free calls can distract your attention from the road.
🚦
Obey all traffic signals and road signs. Red lights and stop signs are non-negotiable safety rules.
🍺
Never drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs. Arrange a designated driver or use public transport.
⏱
Maintain safe following distance. The 3-second rule helps ensure adequate reaction time in emergencies.
🌧
Slow down in poor weather. Rain, fog, and wet roads drastically reduce tyre grip and visibility.
🌙
Use headlights appropriately at night and in low-visibility conditions. Always switch to low-beam in oncoming traffic.
🏍
Two-wheeler riders must wear ISI-certified helmets at all times and ensure passengers do the same.
